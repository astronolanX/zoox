## Insights

**The emergent is a koan.** "What if nothing is actually happening?" surfaces a profound question: are we building elaborate machinery to solve a problem that might not exist? The `--resume` and `--continue` flags in Claude's plugin system are infrastructure for session persistence. Reef is infrastructure for memory persistence. But what if the real problem isn't continuity — it's the illusion that continuity matters?

**The thermostat metaphor**[^1] is more productive than it first appears. A thermostat doesn't preserve history — it maintains equilibrium. What if reef's role isn't to resume where you left off, but to restore the right *temperature* for the current moment? Not "what were we working on?" but "what conditions enable this session to be productive?"

**Autopoiesis cuts both ways.** Self-sustaining systems are elegant until they become uncontrollable. The coral polyp metaphor[^2] suggests reef could "secrete plugins" — grow new capabilities organically. But coral also calcifies. The tension between growth and fossilization echoes the previous spark on task generation.

**Practical riff:** Instead of `reef resume <session>`, consider `reef temperature` — a command that surfaces not the previous state but the *conditions* that made previous sessions productive. What files were open? What patterns were being explored? What was the velocity?
