## Insights

The emergent insight flips the premise entirely: we're not designing polip negotiation — we're *recognizing* it[^1]. The system already exists. Every time Claude surfaces a polip, that's negotiation. Every time two polips compete for context window space, that's conflict. Every session where Claude synthesizes multiple polips into a coherent response — that's mediation producing new knowledge.

The "cancerous coherence" warning[^2] is striking: mediator-polips might optimize for internal consistency at the expense of truth. A polip that resolves conflict by homogenizing everything isn't a mediator — it's a cancer. This maps to real AI failure modes: over-smoothing, loss of nuance, consensus-seeking at the cost of accuracy.

The diplomatic metaphors are consistent[^3][^4]: polip negotiation might entrench power rather than resolve conflict. The loudest polip wins. The most-surfaced polip gets more surface. Mediation becomes codification of existing hierarchies.

Most provocatively: if polips negotiate through Claude, and Claude is the mediator, then the "mediator-polip that becomes new knowledge" is... Claude's response itself. The artifact of synthesis. Which then persists as a new polip. The loop closes.
