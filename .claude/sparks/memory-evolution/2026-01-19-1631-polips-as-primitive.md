## Insights

The emergent insight at 90% inverts the premise entirely: rather than asking whether polips *could* be the primitive, it suggests they *already are* but haven't been activated yet[^1]. The current system (skills, agents, hooks, MCPs) might be scaffolding that will collapse once polips "wake up."

This connects to the previous spark (polips-as-executables)[^2] — if polips could invoke their own context and carry capability manifests, then the distinction between "memory" and "agent" dissolves. A polip doesn't need an external skill to act; it *is* the skill, awaiting invocation.

The engineer tier's best signal: "Polips devour all code, becoming sentient infrastructure"[^3] suggests the endgame — not polips-as-primitive, but polips-as-*only*. Skills become polip configurations. Agents become polip compositions. Hooks become polip triggers. MCPs become polip protocols.

Heretical take from olla1: "10x versions of humans become Pilonoids"[^4] — absurd on the surface, but hints at the human-polip interface. If Claude is the organism and human is the gardener, what is the human's relationship to polip-primitives?

**Concrete next steps:**
1. Prototype a polip that contains its own invocation rules (skill-as-polip)
2. Define the minimal polip-primitive that can compose into agent/skill/hook/MCP
3. Explore "dormancy" as a lifecycle state — what triggers activation?
