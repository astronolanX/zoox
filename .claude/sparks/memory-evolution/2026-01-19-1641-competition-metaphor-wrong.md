## Insights

The emergent insight inverts the question entirely: if knowledge accumulation resembles entropy, then "competition" and "cooperation" are both wrong frames. Entropy doesn't compete or cooperate - it disperses, irreversibly. Knowledge systems might work the same way: not building toward something, but *dissipating* from concentrated ignorance toward distributed understanding.

The highest signal came from groq2 at 85%[^1]: knowledge accumulation is symbiotic, each insight enriching the next. But the emergent synthesis suggests something stranger - symbiosis implies mutual benefit between distinct entities. Entropy implies no entities at all, just gradient dissolution.

The gemin insight at 80%[^2] lands closest: "radical symbiotic accumulation, indifferent to rivalry." The key word is *indifferent*. Competition requires attention to competitors. Accumulation doesn't even notice them.

**Heretical take:** What if the mistake isn't "competition vs cooperation" but "metaphors from living systems"? Reefs are alive. Maybe knowledge isn't. Maybe knowledge is closer to sediment - not growing, not competing, just depositing according to physics we don't yet understand.

**Concrete direction:** The reef project should stop asking "how do polips compete for context?" and start asking "what physics govern knowledge deposition?" If accumulation is entropic, then surfacing polips is *reversing* entropy locally (at the cost of increasing it elsewhere). Surfacing = Maxwell's demon?
